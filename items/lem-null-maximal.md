## Statement

If $(a_n) \in \mathcal{C} \setminus \mathcal{N}$, then the ideal of
$\mathcal{C}$ generated by $\mathcal{N}$ and $(a_n)$ is all of
$\mathcal{C}$. Hence $\mathcal{N}$ is a maximal ideal.

## Facts & Assumptions

**Given:** A Cauchy sequence $(a_n)$ that is not null.

[L1] Away-from-zero: there are $\delta > 0$ and $N_0$ with $|a_n| > \delta$ for all $n \ge N_0$ ([[lem-cauchy-away-from-zero]]).

[L2] Triangle inequality and $|uv| = |u||v|$ ([[lem-rat-triangle]]).

[L3] Cauchy definition and field arithmetic in $\mathbb{Q}$: $\varepsilon\delta^2 > 0$ for $\varepsilon > 0$ ([[thm-rat-ordered-field]]).

[L4] Null definition; a sequence that is $0$ from some index on is null ([[def-null-sequence]]).

[L5] Ideal arithmetic in $\mathcal{C}$: an ideal containing $1$ is the whole ring ([[thm-cauchy-ring]], [[lem-null-ideal]]).

## Proof

**Proof technique:** direct.

1.1 Fix $\delta > 0$ and $N_0$ with $|a_n| > \delta$ for all $n \ge N_0$; in particular $a_n \ne 0$ there. [L1]

2.1 Define $b_n = 1$ for $n < N_0$ and $b_n = 1/a_n$ for $n \ge N_0$. [step 1.1, choose]

3.1 $(b_n)$ is Cauchy: for $m, n \ge N_0$, $|b_m - b_n| = \dfrac{|a_n - a_m|}{|a_m|\,|a_n|} < \dfrac{|a_m - a_n|}{\delta^2}$; given $\varepsilon > 0$, choosing the Cauchy index of $(a_n)$ at $\varepsilon\delta^2$ makes this $< \varepsilon$. [step 2.1, step 1.1, L2, L3]

3.2 For $n \ge N_0$, $a_n b_n = 1$, so the sequence $(a_n b_n - 1)$ is $0$ from $N_0$ on, hence null. [step 2.1, L4]

4.1 Therefore $1_{\mathcal{C}} = (a_n)(b_n) - \bigl((a_n b_n) - 1\bigr)$ lies in the ideal generated by $(a_n)$ and $\mathcal{N}$, so that ideal is all of $\mathcal{C}$; any ideal strictly containing $\mathcal{N}$ contains a non-null element and thus equals $\mathcal{C}$: $\mathcal{N}$ is maximal. [step 3.1, step 3.2, L5] ∎
